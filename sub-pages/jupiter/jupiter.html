Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined

Jupiter's rotation is the fastest of all the Solar System's planets, completing a rotation on its axis in slightly less than ten hours

The Great Red Spot is a storm that is the largest in the Solar System. It produces wind-speeds up to 432 km/h.

Jupiter's upper atmosphere is about 90% hydrogen and 10% helium by volume

Jupiter is a gas giant and has no rocky surface. In roman mythology Jupiter is king of gods.

Jupiter has 80 known natural satellites. Of these, 60 are less than 10 km in diameter. The four largest moons are Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto, collectively known as the "Galilean moons", and are visible from Earth with binoculars on a clear night

Europa is the smallest of the four Galilean moons orbiting Jupiter. It is also the sixth-largest moon in the Solar System. Europa was discovered in 1610 by Galileo Galilei. Europa is the smoothest known object in the Solar System, lacking large-scale features such as mountains and craters. Europa is believed to have under-surface oceans.

Callisto is the second-largest moon of Jupiter, after Ganymede. It is the third-largest moon in the Solar System after Ganymede and Saturn's largest moon Titan, Callisto was discovered in 1610 by Galileo Galilei.

Ganymede is the largest and most massive of the Solar System's moons. The ninth-largest object (including the Sun) of the Solar System

Io is the innermost and third-largest of the four Galilean moons of the planet Jupiter. Slightly larger than Earth's moon, Io is the fourth-largest moon in the Solar System, has the highest density of any moon. It was discovered in 1610 by Galileo Galilei.
