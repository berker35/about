Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine and a half times that of Earth

Saturn is named after the Roman god of wealth and agriculture and father of Jupiter. The Romans named the seventh day of the week Saturday, Sāturni diēs ("Saturn's Day"), for the planet Saturn.

The planet's most notable feature is its prominent ring system, which is composed mainly of ice particles, with a smaller amount of rocky debris and dust.

Saturn is probably best known for the system of planetary rings that makes it visually unique. The rings extend from 6,630 to 120,700 kilometers outward from Saturn's equator and average approximately 20 meters in thickness.

The south pole storm may have been present for billions of years. This vortex is comparable to the size of Earth, and it has winds of 550 km/h.

Saturn has 83 known moons, 53 of which have formal names. In addition, there is evidence of dozens to hundreds of moonlets with diameters of 40-500 meters in Saturn's rings, which are not considered to be true moons.

Titan is the largest moon of Saturn and the second-largest natural satellite in the Solar System. It is the only moon known to have a dense atmosphere

Rhea is the second-largest moon of Saturn and the ninth-largest moon in the Solar System.

Iapetus is the third-largest natural satellite of Saturn and the eleventh-largest in the Solar System.Iapetus was discovered by Giovanni Domenico Cassini, an Italian-born French astronomer, in October 1671.

Dione is a moon of Saturn. It was discovered by Italian astronomer Giovanni Domenico Cassini in 1684. It is named after the Titaness Dione of Greek mythology.

Tethys is a mid-sized moon of Saturn about 1,060 km across. It was discovered by G. D. Cassini in 1684 and is named after the titan Tethys of Greek mythology.

Enceladus is the sixth-largest moon of Saturn (19th largest in the Solar System). It is about 500 kilometers in diameter. Enceladus is mostly covered by fresh, clean ice. Enceladus was discovered on August 28, 1789, by William Herschel

Mimas is a moon of Saturn which was discovered in 1789 by William Herschel. It is named after Mimas, a son of Gaia in Greek mythology.
