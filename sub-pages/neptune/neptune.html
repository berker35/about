Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known solar planet. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet.

It is 17 times the mass of Earth, and slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.

The planet orbits the Sun once every 164.8 years. It is named after the Roman god of the sea

Neptune's weather is characterised by extremely dynamic storm systems, with winds reaching speeds of almost 2,200 km/h.

Triton is the largest natural satellite of the planet Neptune, and was the first Neptunian moon to be discovered, on October 10, 1846, by English astronomer William Lassell. At 2,710 kilometres in diameter, it is the seventh-largest moon in the Solar System.
