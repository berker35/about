Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun and it's roughly half the size of Earth. It is also known as the red planet.

Mars has the highest mountain in the solar system. Olympus Mons is a volcano which has a height of 21.9km

Mars has ice on the north pole and below the surface. Existence of water makes the planet a colonisation candidate.

A martian day is 24 earth hours 37 minutes and a martian year is 687 earth days.

Iron Oxide in the planet's soil gives it the red color.

The two moons of Mars are Phobos and Deimos. They are irregular in shape. Both were discovered by American astronomer Asaph Hall in August 1877 and are named after the Greek mythological twin characters Phobos (fear and panic) and Deimos (terror and dread) who accompanied their father Ares into battle. Ares, god of war, was known to the Romans as Mars.

Phobos is the innermost and larger of the two natural satellites of Mars.

Deimos is the smaller and outermost of the two natural satellites of Mars
